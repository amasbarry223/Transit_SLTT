# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Internal staff of SLTT (Société Traoré de Logistique, Transit et Transport), a
freight/customs transit company in Mali. Five roles use the tool day to day:
Administrateur, Agent de transit, Comptable, Magasinier, Commercial. They work
at a desk (accounting, dossier follow-up) or in a warehouse (stock movements),
in French, handling FCFA amounts, customs paperwork, and client relationships.
The job to be done on any given screen is operational: track a shipment
through its lifecycle (devis → dossier → dédouanement → livraison → solde),
reconcile payments, manage stock, or check today's alerts.

## Product Purpose

A single internal tool replacing paper/spreadsheet tracking for transit
dossiers, accounting entries, invoices, warehouse stock, and supplier costs.
Success looks like: a Comptable can tell in seconds what's overdue, an Agent
de transit can find any dossier's status without asking around, and numbers
shown in different screens are trustworthy and consistent.

## Brand Personality

Sérieux, fiable, professionnel. This is a business tool handling customs
paperwork and money — it should read as precise and dependable, not flashy.
Confidence comes from clarity (correct numbers, unambiguous status) rather
than decoration.

## Anti-references

No specific external anti-reference given. Default guidance: avoid generic
SaaS-dashboard clichés (floating gradient cards, oversized hero metrics,
decorative glassmorphism) in favor of the sober, information-dense register
of a professional back-office tool.

## Design Principles

- Numbers must be scannable and unambiguous — tabular alignment, consistent
  units (FCFA), consistent status color meaning across every screen.
- One visual grammar per repeated pattern (cards, statuses, badges) — no
  ad hoc variations screen to screen.
- Density over decoration: this is a daily-use operational tool, not a
  marketing surface. Prioritize legibility and quick scanning over visual
  flourish.
- Every card earns its size from its content — no filler height/padding
  just to match a neighboring card.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target specified. Maintain existing text-contrast conventions
already used in the app (slate-900 body text, slate-500 secondary text on
white/#F8FAFC backgrounds) and keep interactive elements keyboard operable.
